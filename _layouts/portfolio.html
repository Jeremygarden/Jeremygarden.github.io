Dish+, A food social app with local neighbours

Problem

Nowadays, People especially introvert millenials, are hardly stepping out of door actively to involve socialization. They really want to make friends with others, but there is NO low-cost or just natural way to know someone new. It's not surprising but interesting social psychology phenomenon and which leads to much more social anxienty and causes self-recognition problems later. Apart from those mainstream social media product across various platfroms like FB, Instagram, Snapchat, Tinder and etc. They have a whole lot of viable social features to have people interact so much, but just don't see a similar channel seamlessly integrated back to real life.

Goal

The short-term goal here is to enable location-based social onnections based on home-made food sharing events. The long-term goal here is to resolve monetization strategy (platform ads, feature-based freemium model, partnership with local grocery market and etc) to make this product profitable and sustainable. Figuring out the proper medium is key. It could be achieved via sharing food. So food could become the medium and gather people together naturally. This app is aiming to make socialize connection with local community and nearby neighbours. Why is it? It could be expanded to more target users (Initially will be all Millenials who desire to make friends) after small geographical range of trials on most potential users.

Approach

  • User Login/SignUp
  • Profile
  • Map && NearBy
  • Friends List with ratings
  • Message && Invitation Confirmation
  • Photo Capture

Design Prototype

This was completed by Sketch prototype tool.(Great to learn it!)

A little rough but decide to choose mobile platform layout and light warm coloring themes. There are total 9 pages at this point that are involving user interaction. (Seriously thinking of security verification process as this is a wide open app could be used not for being good.) So It could be 10 ~ 12 pages.

Retrospect

Something needs to be re-considered and also re-assess the growth risk.

  1. As a user, the entire UX experience could be improve and how to ensure unidentified stranger jus sign up and get invited home?
  2. As a business, How many Millenials would like to cook initially even they are going to eat by themselves?
  3. This product just ends up with a starting point as people put aside the product once they are connected. How to maintain the community?

Yelp Semantic Analysis

Goal

The goal here is to estimate the buiness star based on the attributes provided in the raw data(will be "business.json") and also give a relatively accurate semantic results on consumer's review.(will be "review.json")

Approach && Hypothesis

The data input that is originally from Yelp Academic Dataset. With the help of its users, It provides a ealth of information about franchise and vendors. It includes 1,569,264 reviews, spanning from May 2005 to Jan 2015. These reviews are made on 61,184 business records.

Each review contains a one to five stars rating, which indicates customer satisfaction level.

Also the attrivute called "Price Range" is an important player. Because if you pay lots $$$ but only get medium or terrible service, you possibly give the place a bad review. Likewise you pay less than 10 bucks and you get medium service then you might think it's ok exprience. So we would like to drop the data entry if it misses this attribute.

  • Handling Missing values and dummy data
  • Python NTLK Text Classification

By limiting 10,000 records from "review.json"(due to CPU performance concern) as also operate analysis the score of every single review record item.
